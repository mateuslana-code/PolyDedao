ðŸŽ¯ Opportunity Definitions

The system identifies two primary types of market inefficiencies:

Type A Floating Orders (Liquidity Gaps)

A "Floating Order" occurs when the best available price is isolated from the main clusters of liquidity. Ideally, the "valley" is empty.

Figure 1: Real Order Book State (Snapshot 1008).

Detection Logic:

  1. Identify the "Liquidity Mountain" (main concentration of orders).
  2. Check specifically for a "Valley" (gap) between the best price and the mountain.
  3. Trigger Conditions:
    • Gap Size > 2.0% of price
    • Valley Volume < 1.0% of average liquidity
Type B Fat Finger Events

Sudden, large price dislocations caused by large market orders clearing the book.

  • Trigger: Price moves > 5 ticks between snapshots.
  • Verification: Reversion to mean in subsequent seconds.

ðŸ§® Quantitative Methodology

The core strategy utilizes a Sliding Window Density Analysis to detecting liquidity regimes (mountains) vs. anomalies (isolated orders).

1. Liquidity Regime Detection (The Mountain)

We apply a sliding window ($w$) of varying sizes (10%, 15%, 20%, 25% of price span) across the order book to maximize volume concentration density ($D$).

A "Mountain" is defined as a price range $[P_{min}, P_{max}]$ where:

Volume(w) / TotalVolume >= 60%

This effectively identifies the Mode of the market's liquidity distribution, filtering out noise.

2. Anomaly Separation (The Valley)

Once the regime is established, we analyze the area between the Best Price ($P_{best}$) and the Mountain's edge ($P_{edge}$).

We calculate the Valley Density Ratio ($R_D$):

R_D = Density(Valley) / Density(Mountain)

An opportunity is flagged only if $R_D < 0.15$ (The valley is <15% as dense as the mountain), ensuring a statistically significant liquidity gap.

3. Scoring Model

Opportunities are ranked (0-10) using a multi-factor weighted model:

  • Concentration ($\alpha$, 35%): Strength of the mountain (Kurtosis proxy).
  • Emptiness ($\beta$, 30%): Quality of the void (1 - ValleyVolume%).
  • Gap Size ($\gamma$, 20%): Distance in ticks ($P_{edge} - P_{best}$).
  • Profitability ($\delta$, 15%): Absolute USD size of the isolated order.

4. Type B: Fat Finger Detection (Trade Impact)

To detect anomalous market orders ("Fat Fingers") while avoiding false positives from valid breakouts, we use a Percentile-based Relative Impact Model.

Step A: Baseline Establishment
We maintain a rolling window of the last $N=30$ trades to calculate the historical distribution of price impact (in ticks).

Baseline_{impact} = Percentile(History_{impact}, 75th)

Step B: Impact Anomaly Detection
A sequence of trades is flagged if its cumulative price impact ($I_{total}$) significantly exceeds the expected baseline:

I_{total} > 2.5 \times (Baseline_{impact} \times TradeCount)

Step C: Mean Reversion Confirmation
To filter out true market moves (news/breakouts), we require Mean Reversion. The price must revert by at least **50%** of the initial spike within the subsequent 10 trades, returning to a balanced state.
