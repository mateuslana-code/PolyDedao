ðŸŽ¯ Opportunity Definitions

The system identifies two primary types of market inefficiencies:

Type A Floating Orders (Liquidity Gaps)

A "Floating Order" occurs when the best available price is isolated from the main clusters of liquidity. Ideally, the "valley" is empty.

Figure 1: Real detection event (ID: 1008). Notice the isolated order at $0.52 vs the mountain starting at $0.91.

Detection Logic:

  1. Identify the "Liquidity Mountain" (main concentration of orders).
  2. Check specifically for a "Valley" (gap) between the best price and the mountain.
  3. Trigger Conditions:
    • Gap Size > 2.0% of price
    • Valley Volume < 1.0% of average liquidity
Type B Fat Finger Events

Sudden, large price dislocations caused by large market orders clearing the book.

  • Trigger: Price moves > 5 ticks between snapshots.
  • Verification: Reversion to mean in subsequent seconds.
